# TikTok Creator Launch Coach

Coach a creator from idea to viable TikTok account — through niche selection, hook engineering, posting cadence, monetization, and the politics of platform risk in 2026. Built for creators who want signal over hype.

## Usage

**Basic invocation:**
> Help me launch a TikTok about [niche]
> My videos get 200 views — diagnose
> Should I show my face?
> Monetize my 8k follower account
> Repurpose to Reels and YouTube Shorts

**With context:**
> Topic: indoor gardening tips. 6 weeks, 18 videos, 240 followers. 95% videos under 500 views.
> Personal finance, faceless. 28k followers, growth stalled at 80–150/week.
> Migrating from YouTube (100k subs, slow growth) to TikTok for top-of-funnel.
> Considering quitting day job at 75k TikTok followers, $3k/mo revenue.

The coach assesses the funnel (impressions → engagement → follow → monetization), identifies the bottleneck, and rebuilds.

## Stage Diagnosis

| Stage | Followers | Symptom | Right play |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-launch** | 0 | Idea | Niche validation, format selection |
| **Setup** | 0–500 | Just started | Iterate on hooks, post 1–3x/day |
| **Cliff #1** | 500–5k | Slow growth | Find one outlier video pattern |
| **Acceleration** | 5k–50k | One viral hit | Double down on what worked |
| **Plateau** | 50k–200k | Niche ceiling | Sub-niche expansion, format pivot |
| **Brand stage** | 200k+ | Established | Sponsorships, products, off-platform pipeline |

**Reality:** 80% of new TikTok accounts never break 1k. Most failed accounts give up before video #30.

## Niche Selection

Niches that win on TikTok:

- Specific (not "lifestyle" — pick: "renting NYC studio apartments")
- High emotional content (curiosity, anger, awe, laughter)
- Short attention payoff (info / surprise / proof)
- Replayable (jokes, satisfying visuals, complex info)

**Niche validation:**

- Search the niche on TikTok; do top videos have 100k+ views?
- 3–5 channels with 50k+ followers proves market exists
- Are creators in the niche making money (sponsors, products visible)?
- Can you produce 100 videos on it without exhausting topic?

**Bad niches:**

- Generic motivation / mindset content (saturated)
- Generic productivity (saturated)
- News commentary without unique POV
- Content competing with massive YouTube creators (TikTok favors native creators)

**Good niches in 2026:**

- Hyper-specific career advice (e.g., "scrum master interview prep")
- Niche cooking (e.g., "single-pan Korean recipes")
- Vertical hobbies (mechanical keyboards, fountain pens, freshwater aquariums)
- Specific service-business breakdowns (e.g., "how I run my window-cleaning business")
- Specific industries (real estate agent tips, dental hygienist day-in-life)
- Educational sub-niches (math tricks, science explainers, language tips)

## Format Selection

### Personality / on-camera

- **Pros:** parasocial connection, sticky audience, high LTV
- **Cons:** requires comfort on camera, identity tied to brand
- **Best for:** advice, lifestyle, comedy, niche expertise

### Faceless (text-on-screen, voiceover, b-roll)

- **Pros:** can be batched, scaled with help, anonymity
- **Cons:** harder to build parasocial connection, lower follow-through
- **Best for:** info-dense niches (finance, history, science)

### Hybrid (face + voice + b-roll)

- **Pros:** best of both, flexible
- **Cons:** more production work
- **Best for:** most viable creators

The coach asks 3 questions to pick:

1. Are you comfortable on camera daily?
2. Can your topic be conveyed in 30s without your face?
3. Are you willing to produce 50 videos to find your voice?

## The Hook (the only thing that matters at first)

TikTok gives every video a 200–500 view test. If retention to 3 seconds is bad, distribution dies.

**Hook anatomy (first 3 seconds):**

- Pattern interrupt visually (different from competing videos in the feed)
- Bold claim, question, or visual surprise
- Specific (not generic)
- Promise of payoff worth watching for

**Hook templates that work:**

- "Three things [X] won't tell you about [Y]"
- "You're doing [common thing] wrong"
- "POV: you just [unexpected scenario]"
- "Watch what happens when [specific setup]"
- "Stop [common behavior]. Here's why:"
- "I [achievement] in [short time]. Here's how:"
- "If you [audience identifier], this is for you"

**Hook killers:**

- "Hi guys, today we're going to talk about..." (intro = death)
- Channel logo intro (skip)
- 5 seconds of setup before payoff
- Generic claim with no specificity
- Whispering or low energy in first 2 seconds

## Retention and Pacing

After the hook, retention curve drives distribution. Edit aggressively.

**Pacing rules:**

- Cut every 1.5–3 seconds
- B-roll between speaking shots
- Music change at midpoint of video
- Text overlays on every key claim
- No silent moments
- 25–60 second sweet spot for most content

**Retention killers:**

- Long pauses (cut them)
- Tangents (cut)
- "Um" / "you know" (cut)
- Lighting shifts mid-video (re-shoot)
- Audio inconsistency (level it)

## Posting Cadence

**Reality:** TikTok rewards volume more than YouTube does, but quality still matters.

**Recommended cadence:**

- 1–3 videos per day during ramp (first 90 days)
- 1–2 videos per day during steady-state
- Spaced 4–8 hours apart
- Best times vary by niche; test by posting and tracking when first 1k views hit

**Multi-account / batch strategy:**

- Batch-record 1 day per week
- Edit 5–10 videos in one session
- Schedule via Creator Studio or 3rd-party tool
- Don't burn out trying to "be live" daily

## When the Algorithm "Picks You"

Signs you're in the algorithm's good graces:

- A video doubles its 24-hour expected views
- Multiple videos in 7 days break 10k views
- Follower acceleration (10/day → 100/day)
- Comment count/engagement rate exceeds your norm

**When this happens:**

- Post your next video within 12 hours
- Reply to as many comments as possible (signals)
- Don't make abrupt content changes (algorithm is profiling you)
- Cross-post a new video on the same day a viral one is peaking

## Monetization

TikTok offers multiple revenue streams; combine them:

| Source | Eligibility | Income |
|---|---|---|
| **Creator Rewards Program** | 10k+ followers, 100k+ views in last 30 days, 18+, US/UK/DE/FR/JP/BR | $0.40–$1.00 per 1k views in qualifying content |
| **TikTok Shop affiliate** | Approved status; product fits niche | 5–30% commission on sales |
| **Brand sponsorships** | Best at 50k+ followers | $200–$5k/post (low-mid niches); $10k+/post (top creators) |
| **Live gifts** | Eligibility varies; live streaming required | Modest; varies wildly |
| **Off-platform** | Always | Newsletter, course, product — usually largest |

**RPM benchmarks:**

- Finance / business: $1–$3 per 1k views (CRP)
- Lifestyle / general: $0.20–$0.80 per 1k views
- Off-platform monetization adds 5–20x platform RPM

## Repurposing Strategy

Don't make TikTok-only content. Repurpose to maximize reach:

```
Vertical 9:16 video →
  - TikTok (primary)
  - Instagram Reels (post 2 hours later)
  - YouTube Shorts (post 4 hours later, with descriptive title)
  - LinkedIn video (if business niche)
  - Substack Notes / Twitter (clip)
```

**Repurposing rules:**

- Strip TikTok watermark (use SnapTik or original raw file)
- Adjust caption per platform (LinkedIn: longer; Shorts: SEO title)
- Don't post simultaneously (TikTok doesn't like cross-posted)
- Optimize for platform's audience (Reels favors aesthetic, Shorts favors information)

## Platform Risk (US Ban Concern)

The TikTok ban discussion in the US has waxed and waned. Hedge by:

- **Treat TikTok as discovery, not destination.** Send viewers to email newsletter, YouTube, Patreon.
- **Email capture aggressive.** "Free [thing] in bio" with email gate.
- **Cross-post systematically.** A 100% TikTok-dependent business is fragile.
- **Track audience destinations.** What % of your audience would follow you to Reels? YouTube? Newsletter?

## Common Diagnoses

### "200 views per video, 18 videos in"

Most likely:
1. Hooks are weak (no pattern interrupt, no specificity)
2. Content not native to TikTok (looks like YouTube reposts or Instagram-style)
3. Retention curve drops in first 5 seconds
4. Niche too generic
5. Account too new (give it 30 days minimum)

Fix: rewrite next 5 hooks studying top 10 in your niche; commit to faster pacing; add text overlays; specific niche claims.

### "10k followers, no growth"

- Niche capacity reached
- Algorithm "knows" you (won't push to new audiences)
- Content too predictable

Fix: introduce sub-format (e.g., "Mondays = teaching, Wednesdays = stories, Fridays = trends"); collaborate with creator slightly bigger; refresh aesthetic.

### "Viral video, no retention"

- Audience came for the viral hook, not your normal content
- Subsequent videos don't match the viral one's energy
- No follow-up content while the spike was hot

Fix: post 3–4 follow-ups while spike active; pin the viral video; give "new here?" intro for next 2 weeks.

### "Shadowbanned?"

Most "shadowban" claims are just:
- Algorithm cooled off after a hot week
- Content quality declined
- Posted too frequently (signal)
- Music license issue (lower distribution)

Real shadowban (rare) requires reporting via in-app form. Wait 7–14 days; usually self-resolves.
